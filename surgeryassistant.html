Surgical Procedures

Appendectomy

Removal of the appendix to treat appendicitis.

Cholecystectomy

Surgical removal of the gallbladder.

Hernia Repair

Correction of hernias in the abdominal wall.

Cesarean Section

Surgical delivery of a baby through the abdomen.

Coronary Artery Bypass

Bypass surgery to improve blood flow to the heart.

Knee Arthroscopy

Minimally invasive surgery to diagnose and treat knee problems.

Thyroidectomy

Surgical removal of all or part of the thyroid gland.

Mastectomy

Surgical removal of one or both breasts, partially or completely.

Hip Replacement

Replacement of damaged hip joint with prosthetic implant.

Cataract Surgery

Removal of cloudy lens and replacement with artificial intraocular lens.

Colectomy

Surgical removal of all or part of the colon.

Craniotomy

Surgical opening of the skull to access the brain.

Surgical Procedure Detailed Checklists (WHO Best Practices)

Appendectomy Detailed Checklist

  1. Preoperative Preparation: Confirm patient identity, surgical site, and consent. Verify fasting status and review labs.
  2. Patient Positioning: Supine position with arms secured. Apply sequential compression devices to prevent DVT.
  3. Skin Preparation: Cleanse lower right abdomen with antiseptic solution (e.g., chlorhexidine). Allow to dry.
  4. Antibiotic Prophylaxis: Administer recommended prophylactic antibiotics within 60 minutes before incision.
  5. Incision Type: Make a right lower quadrant muscle-splitting incision (e.g., McBurney's or Lanz incision).
  6. Exposure: Retract tissues carefully to expose the appendix. Identify and isolate the appendix.
  7. Appendix Mobilization: Ligate the mesoappendix vessels using clips or ties. Ensure hemostasis.
  8. Appendix Removal: Ligate the base of the appendix with absorbable sutures. Transect and remove the appendix.
  9. Stump Management: Invert the appendiceal stump with purse-string suture if indicated.
  10. Peritoneal Toilet: Irrigate the surgical field with warm saline to reduce infection risk.
  11. Closure: Close muscle layers with absorbable sutures. Close subcutaneous tissue and skin with appropriate sutures or staples.
  12. Postoperative Care: Monitor vital signs, pain control, and wound for signs of infection. Early mobilization encouraged.

Cholecystectomy Detailed Checklist

  1. Preoperative Preparation: Confirm patient identity, consent, and fasting status. Review imaging and labs.
  2. Patient Positioning: Supine with slight reverse Trendelenburg and left tilt to expose gallbladder.
  3. Skin Preparation: Cleanse upper right abdomen with antiseptic solution. Allow to dry.
  4. Antibiotic Prophylaxis: Administer prophylactic antibiotics as per guidelines.
  5. Incision and Access: Create pneumoperitoneum using Veress needle or open technique. Insert trocars under direct vision.
  6. Exposure: Retract the gallbladder fundus cephalad. Identify Calot’s triangle structures.
  7. Dissection: Carefully dissect cystic duct and artery. Achieve critical view of safety.
  8. Clipping and Division: Apply clips to cystic duct and artery. Transect both structures.
  9. Gallbladder Removal: Detach gallbladder from liver bed using electrocautery. Control bleeding.
  10. Specimen Retrieval: Place gallbladder in retrieval bag and remove through trocar site.
  11. Peritoneal Toilet: Irrigate and aspirate any bile or blood.
  12. Closure: Remove trocars under vision. Close fascia for ports >10mm. Close skin with sutures or staples.
  13. Postoperative Care: Monitor vitals, pain, and signs of bile leak or infection. Early ambulation encouraged.

Hernia Repair Detailed Checklist

  1. Preoperative Preparation: Confirm patient identity, consent, and review imaging to classify hernia type.
  2. Patient Positioning: Supine position with arms secured. Apply compression devices.
  3. Skin Preparation: Cleanse surgical site with antiseptic solution. Allow to dry.
  4. Antibiotic Prophylaxis: Administer according to guidelines.
  5. Incision: Make an incision appropriate to hernia location (inguinal, femoral, ventral).
  6. Hernia Sac Identification: Carefully dissect and isolate the hernia sac.
  7. Reduction: Reduce hernia contents back into the abdominal cavity.
  8. Mesh Placement: Place synthetic mesh to reinforce the abdominal wall, ensuring adequate overlap.
  9. Fixation: Secure mesh with sutures or tacks as per technique.
  10. Hemostasis: Ensure bleeding control before closure.
  11. Closure: Close muscle and fascia layers with absorbable sutures. Close skin with sutures or staples.
  12. Postoperative Care: Monitor wound, pain control, and encourage early mobilization. Educate patient on activity restrictions.

Cesarean Section Detailed Checklist

  1. Preoperative Preparation: Confirm patient identity, consent, and fetal monitoring. Verify fasting and labs.
  2. Patient Positioning: Supine with left uterine displacement to avoid aortocaval compression.
  3. Skin Preparation: Cleanse lower abdomen with antiseptic solution. Allow to dry.
  4. Antibiotic Prophylaxis: Administer recommended antibiotics within 60 minutes before incision.
  5. Incision: Make a low transverse (Pfannenstiel) skin incision approximately 2-3 cm above the pubic symphysis.
  6. Layered Dissection: Incise subcutaneous tissue, fascia, and separate rectus muscles to expose peritoneum.
  7. Uterine Incision: Make a low transverse uterine incision. Control bleeding.
  8. Delivery: Deliver the baby carefully, suction airway as needed. Clamp and cut umbilical cord.
  9. Placenta Delivery: Deliver placenta manually or spontaneously. Inspect uterine cavity.
  10. Uterine Closure: Close uterine incision in two layers with absorbable sutures.
  11. Hemostasis and Irrigation: Ensure bleeding control and irrigate abdominal cavity.
  12. Layered Closure: Close peritoneum, fascia, subcutaneous tissue, and skin with appropriate sutures or staple
