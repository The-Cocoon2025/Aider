Surgical Procedures

Appendectomy

Removal of the appendix to treat appendicitis.

Cholecystectomy

Surgical removal of the gallbladder.

Hernia Repair

Correction of hernias in the abdominal wall.

Cesarean Section

Surgical delivery of a baby through the abdomen.

Coronary Artery Bypass

Bypass surgery to improve blood flow to the heart.

Knee Arthroscopy

Minimally invasive surgery to diagnose and treat knee problems.

Thyroidectomy

Surgical removal of all or part of the thyroid gland.

Mastectomy

Surgical removal of one or both breasts, partially or completely.

Hip Replacement

Replacement of damaged hip joint with prosthetic implant.

Cataract Surgery

Removal of cloudy lens and replacement with artificial intraocular lens.

Colectomy

Surgical removal of all or part of the colon.

Craniotomy

Surgical opening of the skull to access the brain.

Detailed Checklists

Appendectomy Detailed Checklist

  1. Materials:
    • Electrocautery device
    • Gauze sponges
    • Hemostats
    • Laparoscopic instruments set
    • Retractors
    • Saline irrigation solution
    • Scalpel
    • Sterile drapes
    • Suction device
    • Sutures and staplers
  2. Preoperative Preparation: Confirm patient identity, surgical site, and consent. Verify fasting status and review labs.
  3. Patient Positioning: Supine position with arms secured. Apply sequential compression devices to prevent DVT.
  4. Skin Preparation: Cleanse lower right abdomen with antiseptic solution (e.g., chlorhexidine). Allow to dry.
  5. Antibiotic Prophylaxis: Administer recommended prophylactic antibiotics within 60 minutes before incision.
  6. Incision Type: Make a right lower quadrant muscle-splitting incision (e.g., McBurney's or Lanz incision).
  7. Exposure: Retract tissues carefully to expose the appendix. Identify and isolate the appendix.
  8. Appendix Mobilization: Ligate the mesoappendix vessels using clips or ties. Ensure hemostasis.
  9. Appendix Removal: Ligate the base of the appendix with absorbable sutures. Transect and remove the appendix.
  10. Stump Management: Invert the appendiceal stump with purse-string suture if indicated.
  11. Peritoneal Toilet: Irrigate the surgical field with warm saline to reduce infection risk.
  12. Closure: Close muscle layers with absorbable sutures. Close subcutaneous tissue and skin with appropriate sutures or staples.
  13. Postoperative Care: Monitor vital signs, pain control, and wound for signs of infection. Early mobilization encouraged.

Cholecystectomy Detailed Checklist

  1. Materials:
    • Electrocautery device
    • Insufflation tubing
    • Laparoscopic clip applier
    • Laparoscopic instruments set
    • Light source and camera cable
    • Retrieval bag
    • Suction device
    • Sutures and staplers
  2. Preoperative Preparation: Confirm patient identity, consent, and fasting status. Review imaging and labs.
  3. Patient Positioning: Supine with slight reverse Trendelenburg and left tilt to expose gallbladder.
  4. Skin Preparation: Cleanse upper right abdomen with antiseptic solution. Allow to dry.
  5. Antibiotic Prophylaxis: Administer prophylactic antibiotics as per guidelines.
  6. Incision and Access: Create pneumoperitoneum using Veress needle or open technique. Insert trocars under direct vision.
  7. Exposure: Retract the gallbladder fundus cephalad. Identify Calot’s triangle structures.
  8. Dissection: Carefully dissect cystic duct and artery. Achieve critical view of safety.
  9. Clipping and Division: Apply clips to cystic duct and artery. Transect both structures.
  10. Gallbladder Removal: Detach gallbladder from liver bed using electrocautery. Control bleeding.
  11. Specimen Retrieval: Place gallbladder in retrieval bag and remove through trocar site.
  12. Peritoneal Toilet: Irrigate and aspirate any bile or blood.
  13. Closure: Remove trocars under vision. Close fascia for ports >10mm. Close skin with sutures or staples.
  14. Postoperative Care: Monitor vitals, pain, and signs of bile leak or infection. Early ambulation encouraged.

Hernia Repair Detailed Checklist

  1. Materials:
    • Laparoscope
    • Electrocautery device
    • Needle holder
    • Scalpel
    • Suction device
    • Surgical mesh
    • Sutures and staplers
    • Tacks or fixation device for mesh
    • Tissue forceps
  2. Preoperative Preparation: Confirm patient identity, consent, and review imaging to classify hernia type.
  3. Patient Positioning: Supine position with arms secured. Apply compression devices.
  4. Skin Preparation: Cleanse surgical site with antiseptic solution. Allow to dry.
  5. Antibiotic Prophylaxis: Administer according to guidelines.
  6. Incision: Make an incision appropriate to hernia location (inguinal, femoral, ventral).
  7. Hernia Sac Identification: Carefully dissect and isolate the hernia sac.
  8. Reduction: Reduce hernia contents back into the abdominal cavity.
  9. Mesh Placement: Place synthetic mesh to reinforce the abdominal wall, ensuring adequate overlap.
  10. Fixation: Secure mesh with sutures or tacks as per technique.
  11. Hemostasis: Ensure bleeding control before closure.
  12. Closure: Close muscle and fascia layers with absorbable sutures. Close skin with sutures or staples.
  13. Postoperative Care: Monitor wound, pain control, and encourage early mobilization. Educate patient on activity restrictions.

Cesarean Section Detailed Checklist

  1. Materials:
    • Blunt scissors
    • Electrocardiogram (ECG) monitor
    • IV infusion set
    • Neonatal resuscitation kit
    • Scalpel
    • Sponge forceps
    • Suction device
    • Surgical drapes
    • Sutures and staplers
    • Umbilical cord clamp
  2. Preoperative Preparation: Confirm patient identity, consent, and fetal monitoring. Verify fasting and labs.
  3. Patient Positioning: Supine with left uterine displacement to avoid aortocaval compression.
  4. Skin Preparation: Cleanse lower abdomen with antiseptic solution. Allow to dry.
  5. Antibiotic Prophylaxis: Administer recommended antibiotics within 60 minutes before incision.
  6. Incision: Make a low transverse (Pfannenstiel) skin incision approximately 2-3 cm above the pubic symphysis.
  7. Layered Dissection: Incise subcutaneous tissue, fascia, and separate rectus muscles to expose peritoneum.
  8. Uterine Incision: Make a low transverse uterine incision. Control bleeding.
  9. Delivery: Deliver the baby carefully, suction airway as needed. Clamp and cut umbilical cord.
  10. Placenta Delivery: Deliver placenta manually or spontaneously. Inspect uterine cavity.
  11. Uterine Closure: Close uterine incision in two layers with absorbable sutures.
  12. Hemostasis and Irrigation: Ensure bleeding control and irrigate abdominal cavity.
  13. Layered Closure: Close peritoneum, fascia, subcutaneous tissue, and skin with appropriate sutures or staples.
  14. Postoperative Care: Monitor maternal vitals, uterine tone, bleeding, and neonatal status. Provide pain management and thromboprophylaxis.

Coronary Artery Bypass Detailed Checklist

  1. Materials:
    • Cardioplegia solution
    • Chest tubes
    • Electrocardiogram (ECG) monitor
    • Heart-lung machine
    • Sternal saw
    • Sternum wires
    • Suction device
    • Surgical drapes
    • Sutures and staplers
    • Vessel dilators
  2. Preoperative Preparation: Confirm patient identity, consent, and cardiac status. Review imaging and labs.
  3. Patient Positioning: Supine with arms extended for IV access and monitoring.
  4. Skin Preparation: Cleanse chest, abdomen, and leg donor sites with antiseptic solution. Allow to dry.
  5. Antibiotic Prophylaxis: Administer antibiotics per protocol.
  6. Incision: Perform median sternotomy to access the heart.
  7. Harvesting Grafts: Harvest saphenous vein or internal mammary artery as grafts.
  8. Cardiopulmonary Bypass: Connect patient to heart-lung machine for extracorporeal circulation.
  9. Coronary Artery Exposure: Identify and prepare coronary arteries for grafting.
  10. Graft Anastomosis: Attach graft vessels to coronary arteries and aorta ensuring patency.
  11. Weaning from Bypass: Gradually restore heart function and remove heart-lung machine support.
  12. Hemostasis and Closure: Control bleeding, place chest tubes, and close sternum with wires. Close soft tissues and skin.
  13. Postoperative Care: Transfer to ICU for cardiac monitoring, ventilation support, and anticoagulation management.

Knee Arthroscopy Detailed Checklist

  1. Materials:
    • Arthroscope and camera system
    • Cannulas
    • Irrigation fluid
    • Meniscus repair kit
    • Shaver system
    • Suction device
    • Sutures and staplers
  2. Preoperative Preparation: Confirm patient identity, consent, and review imaging.
  3. Patient Positioning: Supine with leg secured in a leg holder. Apply tourniquet if indicated.
  4. Skin Preparation: Cleanse knee and surrounding area with antiseptic solution. Allow to dry.
  5. Antibiotic Prophylaxis: Administer antibiotics as per protocol.
  6. Incision and Portals: Make small incisions for arthroscope and instruments (anterolateral and anteromedial portals).
  7. Arthroscope Insertion: Insert arthroscope and establish fluid inflow for joint distension.
  8. Diagnostic Evaluation: Systematically inspect all compartments of the knee joint.
  9. Therapeutic Procedures: Perform indicated procedures such as meniscectomy, ligament repair, or synovectomy.
  10. Hemostasis and Irrigation: Control bleeding and irrigate joint thoroughly.
  11. Closure: Remove instruments, close portals with sutures or steri-strips, and apply sterile dressing.
  12. Postoperative Care: Monitor pain, swelling, and neurovascular status. Initiate early mobilization and physical therapy.

Thyroidectomy Detailed Checklist

  1. Materials:
    • Hemostatic agents
    • Nerve monitoring device
    • Scalpel
    • Small retractors
    • Suction device
    • Sutures and staplers
  2. Preoperative Preparation: Confirm patient identity, consent, and review thyroid function tests and imaging.
  3. Patient Positioning: Supine with neck extended using a shoulder roll.
  4. Skin Preparation: Cleanse anterior neck with antiseptic solution. Allow to dry.
  5. Antibiotic Prophylaxis: Administer antibiotics as per protocol.
  6. Incision: Make a transverse cervical incision two fingerbreadths above the sternal notch.
  7. Dissection: Divide platysma and separate strap muscles to expose thyroid gland.
  8. Identification of Structures: Identify and preserve recurrent laryngeal nerves and parathyroid glands.
  9. Gland Removal: Ligate superior and inferior thyroid vessels. Remove thyroid lobe or total gland as indicated.
  10. Hemostasis: Ensure meticulous bleeding control.
  11. Closure: Reapproximate strap muscles and platysma. Close skin with sutures or staples.
  12. Postoperative Care: Monitor airway patency, calcium levels, and voice changes. Provide pain management.

Mastectomy Detailed Checklist

  1. Materials:
    • Electrocautery pads
    • Gauze and dressings
    • Hemovac drain
    • Scalpel
    • Suction device
    • Sutures and staplers
  2. Preoperative Preparation: Confirm patient identity, consent, and review imaging and labs.
  3. Patient Positioning: Supine with arm abducted on operative side.
  4. Skin Preparation: Cleanse chest wall and axilla with antiseptic solution. Allow to dry.
  5. Antibiotic Prophylaxis: Administer antibiotics as per guidelines.
  6. Incision: Mark and make elliptical incision encompassing breast tissue.
  7. Dissection: Remove breast tissue including nipple-areola complex if indicated.
  8. Axillary Dissection: Perform sentinel lymph node biopsy or axillary clearance as indicated.
  9. Hemostasis: Control bleeding meticulously.
  10. Drain Placement: Place suction drains in surgical site.
  11. Closure: Close skin with sutures or staples.
  12. Postoperative Care: Monitor drains, pain, and wound healing. Provide arm physiotherapy instructions.

Hip Replacement Detailed Checklist

  1. Materials:
    • Bone cement (if needed)
    • Bone saw
    • Implant system (femoral and acetabular components)
    • Reamers and rasps
    • Suction device
    • Sutures and staplers
  2. Preoperative Preparation: Confirm patient identity, consent, and review imaging and labs.
  3. Patient Positioning: Lateral decubitus or supine depending on surgical approach.
  4. Skin Preparation: Cleanse hip and thigh with antiseptic solution. Allow to dry.
  5. Antibiotic Prophylaxis: Administer antibiotics as per protocol.
  6. Incision: Make incision according to chosen approach (posterior, lateral, or anterior).
  7. Dissection: Expose hip joint by dissecting muscles and capsule.
  8. Femoral Head Removal: Dislocate and remove femoral head.
  9. Implant Placement: Prepare acetabulum and femur. Insert prosthetic components.
  10. Stability Check: Assess joint stability and range of motion.
  11. Closure: Close capsule, muscles, subcutaneous tissue, and skin.
  12. Postoperative Care: Monitor vitals, pain, and neurovascular status. Initiate physiotherapy.

Cataract Surgery Detailed Checklist

  1. Materials:
    • Eye speculum
    • Microscope
    • Microsurgical instruments set
    • Sterile drape with eye aperture
    • Suction device
    • Sutures and staplers
    • Viscoelastic device
  2. Preoperative Preparation: Confirm patient identity, consent, and perform ocular measurements.
  3. Patient Positioning: Supine with head stabilized.
  4. Skin and Eye Preparation: Cleanse periocular area with antiseptic solution. Instill topical antibiotics.
  5. Anesthesia: Administer local or topical anesthesia.
  6. Incision: Create small corneal or scleral incision.
  7. Capsulorhexis: Create circular opening in anterior lens capsule.
  8. Phacoemulsification: Fragment and aspirate the cloudy lens.
  9. Intraocular Lens Implantation: Insert artificial lens into capsular bag.
  10. Wound Closure: Hydrate incision or place suture if needed.
  11. Postoperative Care: Monitor intraocular pressure, inflammation, and visual acuity. Prescribe eye drops.

Colectomy Detailed Checklist

  1. Materials:
    • Anastomosis stapler or sutures
    • Bowel clamps
    • Hemostatic agents
    • Scalpel
    • Suction device
    • Surgical drapes
    • Sutures and staplers
  2. Preoperative Preparation: Confirm patient identity, consent, and review imaging and labs.
  3. Patient Positioning: Supine with arms secured.
  4. Skin Preparation: Cleanse abdomen with antiseptic solution. Allow to dry.
  5. Antibiotic Prophylaxis: Administer antibiotics as per protocol.
  6. Incision: Make midline or transverse abdominal incision depending on extent.
  7. Mobilization: Mobilize colon and ligate blood vessels.
  8. Resection: Resect diseased colon segment.
  9. Anastomosis: Perform bowel anastomosis or create stoma as indicated.
  10. Hemostasis: Ensure bleeding control.
  11. Closure: Close abdominal wall layers and skin.
  12. Postoperative Care: Monitor bowel function, pain, and wound healing.

Craniotomy Detailed Checklist

  1. Materials:
    • Bone flap fixation plates
    • Craniotome (drill)
    • Hemostatic agents
    • Microsurgical instruments
    • Sterile drapes and neuro headrest
    • Suction device
    • Sutures and staplers
  2. Preoperative Preparation: Confirm patient identity, consent, and review neuroimaging and labs.
  3. Patient Positioning: Position according to lesion location (supine, lateral, or prone).
  4. Skin Preparation: Cleanse scalp and neck with antiseptic solution. Allow to dry.
  5. Antibiotic Prophylaxis: Administer antibiotics as per protocol.
  6. Incision: Mark and make scalp incision appropriate to craniotomy site.
  7. Scalp Flap Elevation: Elevate scalp flap and expose skull.
  8. Craniotomy: Drill burr holes and remove bone flap.
  9. Dural Opening: Incise dura mater to expose brain.
  10. Surgical Procedure: Perform indicated neurosurgical intervention.
  11. Closure: Close dura, replace bone flap, and close scalp layers.
  12. Postoperative Care: Monitor neurological status, ICP, and wound healing.
